DNS Setup Guide

Configure SPF, DKIM, and DMARC for optimal email deliverability

DNS Setup Guide

Properly configured DNS records are essential for email deliverability. Without them, your emails are far more likely to land in spam or be rejected outright. This guide covers the three key authentication records (SPF, DKIM, DMARC) and the optional MX record for inbox users.

Overview

Email authentication works through DNS records that prove you authorized NorthRelay to send email on behalf of your domain. Receiving mail servers check these records to verify legitimacy.

RecordPurposeRequired
SPFDeclares which servers are allowed to send email for your domainYes
DKIMAdds a cryptographic signature to prove the email was not tampered withYes (auto-configured)
DMARCTells receiving servers what to do with emails that fail SPF/DKIM checksStrongly recommended
MXRoutes inbound email to NorthRelay's mailbox servers (inbox users only)Only for inbox

SPF Record

SPF (Sender Policy Framework) tells receiving mail servers which IP addresses are authorized to send email for your domain.

What to Add

Add a TXT record at the root of your domain:

FieldValue
TypeTXT
Host/Name@ (or leave blank, depending on your DNS provider)
Valuev=spf1 include:_spf.north-pipe.ca -all
TTL3600 (or default)

How It Works

The include:_spf.north-pipe.ca directive tells receiving servers to look up NorthRelay's SPF record, which lists all NorthRelay sending IPs (both shared and sovereign relay servers, IPv4 and IPv6). The -all suffix means "reject email from any other source" — this is the strictest and most secure setting.

If You Already Have an SPF Record

A domain can only have one SPF TXT record. If you already send email through another provider, merge the records:

Do not create a second TXT record starting with v=spf1 — this will cause SPF to fail entirely.

Common Mistakes

  • Using ~all (softfail) instead of -all (hardfail) — softfail is weaker and may not protect you
  • Adding more than 10 DNS lookups in your SPF chain (SPF has a 10-lookup limit)
  • Creating multiple SPF records on the same domain

DKIM Record

DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail) adds a cryptographic signature to each outgoing email, allowing the recipient to verify the message was not altered in transit.

Auto-Configured

DKIM is automatically configured when you verify your domain in the NorthRelay dashboard. After verification, NorthRelay publishes the DKIM public key and signs all outgoing email from your domain.

To verify your domain:

  1. Go to Dashboard → Domains
  2. Click Add Domain and enter your domain name
  3. Add the verification TXT record shown in the dashboard to your DNS
  4. Click Verify
  5. Once verified, DKIM signing is enabled automatically

Verifying DKIM

After domain verification, confirm the DKIM record is published:

You should see a response containing a v=DKIM1; k=rsa; p=... value. If the record is missing, wait up to 48 hours for DNS propagation, then check again.

Subdomains

DKIM signing supports subdomains automatically. If you send from notifications@sub.yourdomain.com, the parent domain's DKIM key will sign the email (using the SubDomains=yes configuration on NorthRelay's signing infrastructure).

DMARC Record

DMARC (Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting, and Conformance) tells receiving servers how to handle emails that fail SPF or DKIM checks, and where to send aggregate reports.

What to Add

Add a TXT record at _dmarc.yourdomain.com:

FieldValue
TypeTXT
Host/Name_dmarc
Valuev=DMARC1; p=quarantine; rua=mailto:dmarc@yourdomain.com
TTL3600 (or default)

Policy Options

PolicyBehaviorRecommended When
p=noneMonitor only, take no actionInitial setup — use this while reviewing reports
p=quarantineSend failing emails to spamAfter confirming SPF and DKIM are working
p=rejectReject failing emails outrightMaximum protection, once you are confident in your setup

We recommend starting with p=quarantine and moving to p=reject after monitoring reports for 2-4 weeks.

Full DMARC Record Example

TagMeaning
p=quarantineQuarantine emails that fail authentication
sp=quarantineSame policy for subdomains
rua=mailto:...Where to send aggregate reports (daily summaries)
ruf=mailto:...Where to send forensic reports (individual failures)
adkim=rRelaxed DKIM alignment (allows subdomain signing)
aspf=rRelaxed SPF alignment
pct=100Apply policy to 100% of failing emails

MX Record (Inbox Users)

If you are on the Startup tier or above and want to receive email through NorthRelay's inbox, add an MX record pointing to NorthRelay's mailbox server.

FieldValue
TypeMX
Host/Name@ (or leave blank)
Valuemx.northrelay.ca
Priority10
TTL3600 (or default)

This routes inbound email for your domain to NorthRelay's mailbox servers, where it is accessible via the Dashboard inbox or IMAP/JMAP clients.

Using the Dashboard

Go to Dashboard → Domains, select your domain, and click Check DNS. The dashboard will verify all required records and report any issues.

DNS Provider Examples

Cloudflare

  1. Log in to Cloudflare and select your domain
  2. Go to DNS → Records → Add Record
  3. Set type to TXT, name to @, and paste the SPF value
  4. Repeat for DMARC (name: _dmarc)
  5. If using proxy (orange cloud), note that TXT records are never proxied

Amazon Route 53

  1. Open the Route 53 console and select your hosted zone
  2. Click Create Record
  3. Leave the record name blank for root, set type to TXT
  4. Paste the SPF value in quotes: "v=spf1 include:_spf.north-pipe.ca -all"
  5. Route 53 requires TXT values to be enclosed in double quotes

GoDaddy

  1. Go to My Products → DNS for your domain
  2. Click Add under the Records section
  3. Select type TXT, set host to @, and paste the SPF value
  4. GoDaddy may take up to 48 hours to propagate changes

Troubleshooting

DNS propagation delays

DNS changes can take up to 48 hours to propagate globally, though most providers update within 1-4 hours.

  • Use dnschecker.org to check propagation status worldwide
  • Lower your TTL to 300 (5 minutes) before making changes to speed up future updates

Conflicting SPF records

If you have multiple TXT records starting with v=spf1, SPF validation will fail. Merge all include: directives into a single record.

"Too many DNS lookups" SPF error

SPF allows a maximum of 10 DNS lookups. Each include: directive counts as one lookup. If you exceed 10, consider:

  • Removing providers you no longer use
  • Using ip4: or ip6: directives instead of include: for static IPs
  • Using an SPF flattening service

DKIM record not found

  • Wait up to 48 hours after domain verification
  • Confirm you verified the domain in Dashboard → Domains
  • Check the exact record name: default._domainkey.yourdomain.com (not dkim._domainkey)

DMARC reports not arriving

  • Verify the rua email address is correct and can receive email
  • Some providers take 24-48 hours to start sending reports
  • Check that the rua address domain has a DMARC report authorization record if it differs from the sending domain
